<p><p>The all-weather ski wax market is primarily divided into cold wax and hot wax types. Cold wax is designed for lower temperatures, providing optimal glide and protection in icy conditions, making it suitable for less aggressive skiing. In contrast, hot wax is applied using a waxing iron, offering superior performance and durability across various temperatures, ideal for competitive skiing and challenging snow conditions. Both types enhance ski performance, but their application and effectiveness vary based on environmental conditions.</p></p>
